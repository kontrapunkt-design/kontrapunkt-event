How to build a digital brand experience in-store

Technology is on the move – and so are your customers. Join our morning workshop 8th of May from 9 - 11, where we highlight emerging digital consumer trends and explore the future of retail with hands-on experiences in our tech lab.

Get your hands on in our tech lab

  • Beacon technology

    Try the Beacon technology, a micro-location-based technology allowing you to create customised store experiences – Starbucks in the US even use Beacon for coffee orders. In Germany, consumers are offered in-store special offers and gain bonus points when using the Beacon-generated shopstar app, which also provides store owners valuable data on shopping habits.

  • Virtual reality

    Explore simulations of real-world environments. The tech lab will include head-mounted Oculus Rift, the Epson Moverio motion tracker – and the Samsung Gear VR and Cardboard that transforms a cell phone into a VR headset. The technology can be used to create multi-sensory brand-related experiences, such as Wrigley: 5Gum, which lets gamers taste, smell and sense five chewing gum labels.

  • Augmented reality

    Experience direct or indirect visions of a physical, real-world environment featuring elements augmented (or supplemented) by computer-generated sensory input. The technology can be used on devices to localise businesses and offer retail experiences – such as with LEGO kiosks that allow shoppers a virtual preview of assembled LEGO boxes.

  • Touchless application

    Discover touchless technologies, which will include Leap Motion, a 3D controller tracking hand and finger movements and the Kinect hands-free technology that can recognise specific gestures. The technology was used by ETT Solutions when they designedg the Samsung Smart Home in Milan and can be applied as a virtual fitting room, allowing consumers to try out garments from many different stores.
